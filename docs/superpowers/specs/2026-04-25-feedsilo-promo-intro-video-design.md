# FeedSilo Promo Intro Video Design

Date: 2026-04-25

## Goal

Create a 30-60 second Remotion intro video script for FeedSilo. The video should feel founder-led, practical, and honest. It should explain why FeedSilo exists, what it does today, and why a technical or research-heavy user should run it themselves.

## Audience

The primary audience is creators, researchers, AI builders, founders, and other power users who save useful material from feeds and want to find it again later. The secondary audience is developers and self-hosters who care about open source, data ownership, and extensibility.

## Positioning

FeedSilo is an open-source, self-hosted archive for the parts of the internet worth keeping.

The emotional hook is that feeds can contain excellent information, but they are also noisy, negative, and designed to keep people scrolling. FeedSilo pulls the good stuff out of that environment and stores it in a private, searchable library.

## Tone

The tone should be founder-calm:

- Personal enough to explain why FeedSilo exists.
- Specific enough to feel credible.
- Polished enough for a launch video.
- Clear that the current CTA is open-source/self-hosted, while future discovery and cloud features are roadmap direction.

## Recommended Runtime

Target 45-55 seconds. This is long enough to include the origin story and the product promise without feeling padded.

## Voiceover Script

I built FeedSilo because I was tired of losing the good parts of the internet inside noisy feeds.

Twitter especially can be exhausting: great ideas, useful threads, research, prompts, and articles, all mixed in with arguments, negativity, and the pressure to keep scrolling.

FeedSilo is my way of saving the useful parts before they disappear back into the scroll.

It lets you capture posts, threads, articles, RSS items, prompts, and media into a private library you control.

Then you can search it by keyword or meaning, open the original context, filter by source or topic, and actually find the thing you saved.

Today, FeedSilo is open source and self-hosted.

Run it yourself, own your archive, and help shape where it goes next.

## 30-Second Cutdown

I built FeedSilo because I was tired of losing useful ideas inside noisy feeds.

It is an open-source, self-hosted archive for the internet you actually want to keep.

Capture posts, threads, articles, RSS items, prompts, and media into one private library.

Then search by keyword or meaning, filter by topic, and find the context again when you need it.

FeedSilo helps you save the good stuff without living inside the scroll.

Run it yourself, own your archive, and help shape what comes next.

## Visual Structure

1. 0-7 seconds: Problem and origin
   - Show a quick, abstract chaotic-feed-to-calm-library opening or a subdued feed/search visual.
   - On-screen text: "Save the good stuff."

2. 7-15 seconds: Product reveal
   - Show the FeedSilo home/dashboard with saved items.
   - On-screen text: "A self-hosted archive for the internet worth keeping."

3. 15-25 seconds: Capture
   - Show browser capture, saved posts, threads, articles, RSS, prompts, and media.
   - Emphasize that content lands in one private library.

4. 25-40 seconds: Rediscover
   - Show search, semantic results, filters, pinned topics, and detail context.
   - The visual proof should be "I can actually find the thing I saved."

5. 40-50 seconds: Ownership and CTA
   - Show settings/export/GitHub or the README.
   - End on open source and self-hosted ownership.

## Remotion Notes

- Build the promo intro as a fresh Remotion project/composition rather than reusing the existing `demo-video` files.
- Keep the existing `demo-video` folder untouched except as optional reference for available product screenshots or prior timing ideas.
- Favor real FeedSilo UI screenshots or recordings over fake UI.
- Keep motion calm: slow pans, small zooms, soft transitions, and concise type overlays.
- Avoid promising automated discovery as a current feature. Refer to it only as direction if it appears at all.
- Voiceover should lead timing. Music, if used, should sit underneath the narration and stay quiet.

## Success Criteria

- A new viewer understands FeedSilo in under 30 seconds.
- The origin story feels true without taking over the video.
- The current product claim is accurate: capture, archive, search, filter, own.
- The CTA is clear: open source, self-hosted, run it yourself.
