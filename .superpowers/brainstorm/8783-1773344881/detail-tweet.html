I've been playing around with a new approach to RL from Human Feedback that uses a much simpler reward signal. Instead of training a separate reward model, you can directly use the LLM's own log probabilities as a proxy for quality.

The key insight is that if you prompt the model with "Rate this response from 1-10:" and look at the token probabilities for each number, you get a surprisingly good reward signal that correlates well with human preferences.
Chart: RLHF comparison results
Here's the config I've been using — feel free to try it yourself:
1{
2 "model": "gpt-4-turbo",
3 "temperature": 0.7,
4 "max_tokens": 4096,
5 "reward_method": "self_logprob",
6 "num_samples": 16,
7 "use_kl_penalty": true,
8 "kl_coef": 0.05,
9 "baseline": null
10}
The results are surprisingly competitive with full RLHF — within ~2% on most benchmarks. Plus setup takes minutes instead of days. Thread below with more details.
